Snakes on a Plane inside Malls on top of America



Someone, not me, opened this document
And typed:
There are larva in my mom’s raisin bran(d)

But anyways, I have died as a moth.
I have hibernated for one hundred thousand years
and lived to tell the tale to you
A tale of breaking bread in a Town Mall

We ate at Panera bread on a Sunday ‘eve,
But there is a certain conversation lost
My dear, I have lived another two hundred thousand years

My child, In the Sycamore mall, we saw X-men III
There is an un-writable feeling I must write to stay true
Nobody remains to register who had been lost in the light
Not northern lights, nor the natural law
Could make me afraid to dream of Genosha

After watching X-men, we walked
Through Target’s bright lights and onto Panera Bread
Squatting and loitering on a stool, nearby a stall,
We laughed at the 40-year-old Panda Expresser
The Cold-stoner sucker fish

I realized the contrast between X-men and Target
It reminds me of how we had our talk
We talked out right, because there was no one
The lights flickered on and off
On was X-men. Off was Target. Panera is in-between

When I watch X-men it feels like anything can happen,
Walking around Target is the opposite
Set so close together, Orange Julius bridges the gap
Lordy thank God for bridges
